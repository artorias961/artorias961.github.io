Chapter 1: Statistic and Significance

What is Statistics? What is Computation? How are they similar and/or different?
First, statistics is a branch of mathematics that deals with the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data. It is used to make inferences and decisions based on data.
Second, computation refers to the process of using a computer or other machine to perform mathematical calculations. It can also refer to the process of using algorithms to solve problems and make decisions.
Finally, both statistics and computation involve using mathematical methods to process and analyze data, but they are typically used for different purposes. Statistics is used to make inferences and draw conclusions about a population based on a sample of data, whereas computation is used to perform specific calculations or solve problems.
